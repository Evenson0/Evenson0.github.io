I am an actuarial analyst in property and casualty insurance, with interests at the intersection of mathematics, actuarial science, and computation.

My academic path has moved across several disciplines. I studied mathematics with a specialization in actuarial science at the Université de Montréal, while also pursuing software engineering at Polytechnique Montréal with an emphasis on artificial intelligence and data science. Before coming to Canada, I completed a degree in electromechanical engineering in Haiti.

My professional work is centered on pricing, modeling, and the development of actuarial tools. I am especially drawn to the technical side of quantitative work: translating mathematical ideas into code, structuring data for analysis, and building systems that make complex reasoning more usable in practice.

This website is a place where I think in public. Here I write about actuarial science, quantitative finance, and both pure and applied mathematics. Some posts are theoretical, others are computational, and others are simply attempts to understand a problem more clearly by working through it carefully.

Over time, I hope this site becomes a record of sustained intellectual work: a space where technical ideas, mathematical curiosity, and long-term ambition meet.

Outside of industry work, I have remained interested in teaching, writing, and the communication of mathematics. I have worked as a teaching assistant in mathematics and statistics, contributed articles to the student journal *L’Axiomatique*, and I continue to look for ways to connect rigorous ideas with practical applications.

If your interests include actuarial modeling, stochastic processes, financial mathematics, or computational approaches to quantitative problems, you may find something of interest here.
